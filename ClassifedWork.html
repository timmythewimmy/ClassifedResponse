Penalex Complicity Code — Filing Reference PX-117

This document is issued as a formal judicial-style record concerning compliance, liability, and enforcement expectations related to the facilitation or support of unlawful activity.

Article I — Jurisdiction and Authority

Under the authority of the Penalex Complicity Code, oversight bodies retain full discretion to evaluate, flag, and process individuals suspected of indirect or direct assistance to illegal actions.

Jurisdiction applies universally and without exception once involvement criteria have been met.

Article II — Definition of Complicity

Complicity is defined as any action, communication, or omission that enables, encourages, protects, conceals, or otherwise supports illegal conduct.

This includes advisory input, logistical coordination, digital communication, emotional reinforcement, or passive acknowledgment.

Article III — Severity Classification

Any verified instance shall be classified as Penalex Complicity — Tier One.

Tier One classification denotes maximum seriousness and is treated as equivalent to direct participation regardless of stated intent.

Article IV — Enforcement Procedure

Upon classification, enforcement review may be initiated immediately. Subjects may be placed under restriction, monitoring, or containment without advance notice.

Procedural escalation does not require consent, acknowledgment, or cooperation from the subject.

Article V — Defense Limitations

The following claims are invalid under this Code: ignorance, humor, loyalty, friendship, coercion, emotional response, or lack of awareness.

Statements minimizing involvement shall be recorded but do not affect liability.

Article VI — Record Consequences

Penalex Complicity designation may result in permanent record annotation and extended enforcement oversight.

Removal of designation is rare and subject to discretionary review.

Article VII — Final Advisory

Continued involvement after awareness of this record constitutes aggravation and may result in escalated containment measures.

Noncompliance is not negotiable.
